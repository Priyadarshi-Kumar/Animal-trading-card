The Bengal Tiger

The Bengal tiger, also known as the Royal Bengal Tiger or the Indian tiger, is the subspecies with the largest population. It is the national animal of India, place where its image is part of the traditions and the culture.

  • Habitat: Tropical Rainforest
  • Average Lifespan: 20 Years
  • Average Size: 90-100 cm
  • Runnig Speed: 50-65 Kmph

The Bengal tiger is found primarily in India with smaller populations in Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan, China and Myanmar. It is the most numerous of all tiger subspecies with more than 2,500 left in the wild.
